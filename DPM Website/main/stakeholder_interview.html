Stakeholder Interviews

The following interviews were conducted as part of our research into dance performances
in the Mangaung Community. They were designed to capture both the organizational
background and the personal experiences of our stakeholders.

Interview Guide

Main Questions

  1. Briefly explain the historical background of your organization.
  2. What is the goal/purpose of your organization?
  3. What cultural activities or events do you organize mostly?
  4. Which dances do you mainly focus on?
  5. What do you enjoy most about being part of this organization?

Follow up Questions

  1. What challenges do you face in promoting your cultural activities?
  2. If someone wanted to get involved, what's the easiest way to start?

Stakeholder 1

Name:Luyolo
Role/Title:Chairperson
Organization:CUT Xhosa Society
Language of Interview: Xhosa

Luyolo serves as the Chairperson of CUT Xhosa Society. In this role, they oversee the
organization's activities and play a leading part in preserving and promoting cultural dance
within the Mangaung Community. Their leadership has been central to keeping these
traditions alive and accessible to the community.

Key Takeaways

  • The organization has been actively preserving Xhosa cultural heritage in the
    Mangaung Community for a number of years, with a strong focus on education and
    community involvement.
  • Their main dance focus is traditional Xhosa dance, which is performed at cultural
    celebrations, school events, and community gatherings.
  • One of their biggest challenges is securing funding to sustain their activities and reach
    a wider audience.

Stakeholder 2

Name:Asiphe
Role/Title:Chairperson
Organization:CUT Zulu Society
Language of Interview: Zulu

Asiphe is the Chairperson of CUT Zulu Society. They are dedicated
to celebrating and safeguarding the cultural heritage of the Mangaung Community through
dance and organized cultural events. Under their guidance, the organization continues to
make a meaningful impact on local culture.

Key Takeaways

  • The organization was established with the goal of keeping Zulu cultural traditions alive
    among the younger generation in the Mangaung Community.
  • They primarily focus on traditional Zulu dance, including performances at cultural
    festivals and community events.
  • A key challenge they face is attracting youth participation, as many young people are
    not as connected to their cultural roots as previous generations were.
